the old holy trinity

The Ferrari F40, unveiled in 1987, is a legendary supercar designed to celebrate Ferrari's 40th anniversary. Renowned for its lightweight design, raw performance, and a twin-turbocharged V8 engine, it remains an icon of automotive excellence.

The McLaren F1 is a legendary supercar introduced in 1992, renowned for its central driving position, and unmatched engineering. Powered by a BMW-sourced 6.1L V12 engine, it held the record for the fastest production car for over a decade, reaching 240 mph.

The Porsche Carrera GT is a high-performance mid-engine supercar, celebrated for its naturally aspirated 5.7-liter V10 engine, delivering 603 horsepower. Known for its precise engineering and timeless design, it remains an icon among automotive enthusiasts.

Gt40

Ford Gt40

The Ford GT40 is an iconic race car powered by a range of V8 engines, depending on the model, with outputs exceeding 400 hp. Designed todominate endurance racing, it features a lightweight body and mid-engine layout for optimal balance. With a top speed over 320 km/h (200 mph),it achieved legendary status by winning the 24 Hours of Le Mans four times consecutively (1966–1969). Its aerodynamic design, low profile, and racing pedigree make it a timeless symbol of performance and innovation.

CLK GTR

The Mercedes-Benz CLK GTR is a rare homologation hypercar powered by a 6.9-liter V12 engine producing 604 hp and 775 Nm of torque. It accelerates from 0–100 km/h in 3.8 seconds, with a top speed of 344 km/h (214 mph). Built on a lightweight carbon-fiber monocoque, it features a mid-engine layout, race-inspired suspension, and aerodynamics optimized for downforce. With only 25 road cars produced, it combines race car performance with minimal luxury, rooted in its origins as a Le Mans GT1 competitor.

the new holy trinity

The Ferrari LaFerrari is a hybrid hypercar that combines a 6.3-liter V12 engine with an electric motor, producing a combined 950 horsepower for blistering speed and performance. As Ferrari's first hybrid, it showcases cutting-edge technology and aerodynamic design.

The McLaren P1 is a hybrid hypercar combining a 3.8L twin-turbo V8 engine with an electric motor to produce a total of 903 horsepower, achieving a top speed of 217 mph. Built with an aerodynamic carbon-fiber body and advanced suspension, it delivers exceptional track and road performance.

The Porsche 918 Spyder is a high-performance plug-in hybrid hypercar, combining a 4.6L naturally aspirated V8 engine with two electric motors for a total output of 887 horsepower. Built with and lightweight carbon-fiber construction, it achieves remarkable speed and efficiency.

Agera RS

Agera RS

The Koenigsegg Agera RS is a hypercar powered by a 5.0-liter twin-turbo V8 engine, delivering up to 1,341 hp and 1,371 Nm of torque. It accelerates from 0–100 km/h in 2.9 seconds and set a top speed record of 447 km/h (277 mph). Built with a lightweight carbon fiber monocoque, it features advanced aerodynamics, active rear wing, and a 7-speed automated manual transmission. With track-focused handling and bespoke customization options, the Agera RS is a benchmark in speed, innovation, and exclusivity.
